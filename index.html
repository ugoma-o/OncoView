Show the anatomy. Explain the radiation. Reduce uncertainty.

OncoGuide 3D is a clinic-friendly radiation oncology education workspace for demonstrating cancer treatment sites and making treatment conversations more visual, structured, and easier for patients to understand.

Clinicians can open an embedded 3D anatomy model viewer, then walk through treatment logistics, setup previews, dose explanations, expected side effects, symptom timelines, practical supportive care, and clinician note templates in a static, privacy-conscious web format.

Clinic workflow

Select an organ system, choose a disease site, open the relevant 3D model, then walk through treatment setup, dose and fractionation, expected symptoms, and practical supportive care.

What radiation is doing

Radiation uses targeted high-energy beams to treat tumor cells while limiting dose to nearby normal tissues whenever possible.

What the patient should expect

Treatment usually begins with consultation, simulation, immobilization, planning, quality assurance, then daily or near-daily treatment visits depending on the regimen.

    Paste the iframe src or standalone published viewer URL for each disease site. This is saved only in this browser through localStorage.

    Tip: In BioDigital, publish the model, choose responsive embed if available, copy the iframe code, then paste only the src URL here. If authentication fails, check that your BioDigital developer key is authorized for your GitHub Pages domain.
